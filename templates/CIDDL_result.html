LIME Explanation

The image on the right represents the input image that was processed by our model. To help you understand the model's decision-making process, we've generated a LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) visualization. LIME is a technique that aims to illuminate the 'why' behind a model's predictions by highlighting the parts of the input that were most influential. In this visualization, the colored regions on the image indicate the areas that the model focused on when making its determination. The intensity of the color corresponds to the degree of influence; brighter colors signify areas that had a stronger impact on the model's output. Essentially, LIME works by creating numerous slightly altered versions of the original image and observing how the model's predictions change in response. By analyzing these changes, it identifies the features that are most critical to the model's decision. It's important to remember that LIME provides a local explanation, meaning it's specific to this particular image and the model's behavior for this instance, rather than a global explanation of the model's overall logic. This visualization serves as a valuable tool for understanding which features the model deemed significant, offering insights into how it arrived at its conclusion.
